Module 2: Organizational Readiness

CIA Triad

Confidentiality (Accessibility)

  • Ensuring info is accessible ONLY to those authorized
  • Protection from unauthorized access and use
  • Protecting data on systems, in transit, in process

Integrity

  • Safeguarding accuracy and completeness of info
  • Detection of alterations in storage, transit, process

Availability

  • Ensuring authorized users have access when required
  • Acceptable level of performance
  • Fault tolerance
  • Prevention of data loss & destruction
  • Reliable backups, redundancy
Before Emergency

Leadership → Commitment, Support, Funding, Approval


Who manages? Different emergencies = different managers?

  • Power outage?
  • Network disruption?
  • Transportation failure?
  • Medical quarantine?
  • Civil protest?

Author / Editor / Owner:

  • Who writes CP?
  • Who updates?
  • Who ensures compliance?
Measurable Metrics
MTD Maximum Tolerable Downtime
RTO Recovery Time Objective
RPO Recovery Point Objective
BIA (Business Impact Analysis)
  • Identify critical business functions
  • Prioritize by importance
  • Data Collection: right people, questionnaires, facilitated sessions, risk assessments, system logs, financial reports

Key Terms

Asset Organizational resource being protected (logical/physical)
Threat Object, person, or entity that is a potential risk of loss to an asset
Threat-Agent Specific/identifiable instance of a general threat that exploits vulnerabilities
Vulnerability Flaw in system, security procedures, design that could result in breach
Well-Known Vulnerabilities Examined, documented, published
Exploit Using system/info illegally for personal gain; can target specific vulnerability
Control/Safeguard/Countermeasure Security mechanisms, policies, procedures that counter attacks, reduce risk
Trespass Electronic/human activities that breach confidentiality

Chapter 3: Contingency Strategies

Backup Types
Differential All files changed since last FULL backup
Incremental Only files modified since last backup (shortest time, least space, often daily)
Copy Backup of specified files regardless of modification
Virtualization
Host Platform Physical server & OS that virtualization app & VMs run on
Hypervisor Virtualization app/software that enables VMs on host
Virtual Machine Virtualized environment running on host platform
BC vs DR
Business Continuity Steps for implementing critical functions using alternate mechanisms until normal ops resume
Disaster Recovery Efforts to resume normal operations at primary place of business

Chapter 4: IR Planning

Flow: CMPT → IRPC
  • CMPT passes BIA to IRPC
  • IRPC gives info to subcommittees:
    • Incident Response (CSIRT)
    • Business Continuity
    • Disaster Recovery
IRPC Policy Must Be:
  • Leadership Support
  • Clarity
  • Enforceable
  • Required/Sufficient length
  • Functional
  • Realistic
Incident Timeline

Before Incident — Training CSIRT:

  • Select tools/techniques
  • SANS Institute / DHS / US CERT conferences
  • Professional reading program

During Incident

  • Who is in charge?

After Incident — MIR (Major Incident Review):

  • Who is involved?
  • What happened?
  • How did it happen?
  • Can we prevent it?
Plan, Then Test the Plan!
  • Develop multiple plans
  • Leadership chooses best fit + feedback
  • Desk Check
  • Structured Walk-through (table reads)
  • Simulation (practice drills / dry runs)
  • Parallel Testing (rehearsals)
  • Full Interruption
  • War Gaming
  • Repeat on schedule
SETA — Security Education, Awareness, Training
  • What is expected
  • How to recognize an attack
  • How to report suspected incident
  • How to mitigate damage

Good Security Practices:

  • OS patched
  • Antiviruses
  • Operating in least privilege
  • Not opening suspect attachments
  • Avoiding social engineering
  • Not downloading unauthorized software
  • Using secure passwords
  • Utilizing company VPN

Chapter 5: Building CSIRT

Acronyms
  • CSIRT — Computer Security Incident Response Team
  • SIRT — Security Incident Response Team
  • CERT — Computer Emergency Response Team
Steps to Build CSIRT
  • Obtain management support & buy-in
  • Determine CSIRT strategic plan
  • Gather relevant information
  • Design the CERT vision
  • Communicate CSIRT vision & operational plan
  • Begin implementation
1. Obtaining Buy-in

Buy-in = "Acceptance of and willingness to actively support & participate"


Budget: Project startup vs annual ops, preliminary vs additional

2. Strategic Plan
  • Time frame
  • Gap analysis
  • Training methods
  • Testing requirements
  • Formal & informal communications
  • Updating documents
Structure & Model

Sourcing: Central / Distributed / Coordinating

Staffing: Employees / Partially outsourced / Fully outsourced

Team Model: 24/7 monitoring / Full-time vs part-time / Employee morale

Typical CSIRT Skill Sets
  • Malware: Scanning, elimination, recovery
  • System Administration
  • Network Admin: Switches, routers, recovery
  • Firewall Administration
  • Intrusion Detection Systems
  • Cryptography
  • Data Storage & Recovery (RAID)
  • Data Networks
  • Documentation: Creation & maintenance
4. Designing the CSIRT Vision
  • Identify your constituency: Who/what does it support?
  • Define CSIRT's mission, goals, objectives
  • Determine organizational model
  • Select services it will provide
  • Identify required resources (staff, equipment, infrastructure)
  • Determine funding

Protect & Forget vs Apprehend & Prosecute

Protect & Forget
  • Near incident
  • Terminate invasion
  • How access obtained
  • Restore systems
  • Secure method
  • Document steps
  • Lessons learned
  • Management eval
Apprehend & Prosecute
  • Near incident
  • Contact law enforcement
  • Document actions, time, who
  • Isolate systems
  • Monitor intruder
  • Discover identity
  • Discover access method
  • Enable invasion
  • Document state
  • Restore systems
  • Secure method
  • Document Q&A
  • Secure all logs
  • Lessons learned
  • Management eval

Other CSIRT Services

  • Incident detection & notification
  • Managing Incidents:
    • Incident Analysis
    • Incident response on site
    • Incident response support
    • Incident response coordination
  • Managing Vulnerabilities:
    • Vulnerability Analysis
    • Vulnerability response
    • Vulnerability response coordination
Preparation
  • Assessment Activities
  • Managing Configuration across the enterprise:
    • Frameworks
    • Tools
    • Applications
  • Managing Intrusion Detection
  • Security Event & Information Management (SEIM) activities
Quality Assurance
  • Analyzing risk
  • Business resumption planning activities
  • Security Consulting
  • Ongoing awareness, education, and training programs
  • Evaluation / certification of purchased and developed tools
